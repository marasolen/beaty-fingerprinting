Beaty Biodiversity Museum: Fingerprints

For each row, there is a "Petri dish" and a set of vials. The Petri dish itself is red if I identified a specimen that I interpreted as dangerous in the row, and grey otherwise. The "organisms" in the Petri dishes represent groups of specimens in the row. There are three attributes: size, aquatic to terrestrial, and flies vs doesn't fly. Each organism is a specific combination of these three attributes. For example, one organism may represent Small Terrestrial organisms that can Fly. The size of the organism represents how many specimens match that combination of attributes within the row, and the colour gradient represents the attributes, with size in the centre, ability to fly on the outside, and aquatic to terrestrial in between. Below the Petri dish is a set of "vials" which is essentially a bar chart of the counts of the different attributes within the row, and acts as a sort of legend for the organisms as well. I also kept track of how many of each organism's specimens were images vs objects, and whether the row had any "hidden" specimens (ie specimens in drawers), but I haven't visually encoded either of those things yet.
